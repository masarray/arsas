Suggested workflow

Find the mismatch before editing the project.

Interoperability problems often involve differences between configured intent, live server behavior and the expectations of the receiving SAS or gateway.

01

Open project context

Load the available SCL and identify the intended IED, access point, DataSets, RCBs, GOOSE control blocks and addresses.

02

Discover the live IED

Connect to the approved endpoint and inspect the model, services, DataSets, report controls and command descriptors actually exposed.

03

Compare reporting evidence

Check RCB availability, ownership, DataSet membership, report behavior and any rejected or unsupported operations.

04

Inspect GOOSE identity

Verify APPID, VLAN, destination MAC, goCBRef, DataSet reference, sequence counters and ordered payload leaves.

05

Prepare a controlled baseline

Select the intended RCB, retain verified DataSet members and export an Edition 1 or Edition 2 SCL baseline for further engineering review.

Boundary

Evidence is not universal conformance.

A successful connection or export does not prove full IEC 61850 conformance, interoperability with every vendor, project correctness or acceptance by the receiving system. Final engineering review remains required.
